A Game to Remember: As a lifelong Manchester United fan from the US, a childhood dream of mine came true last January when I visited England to watch the Manchester Darby at Old Trafford. The Manchester Darby is a huge footballing event between Manchester City, concidered to be the best club team in the world right now, and Manchester United, which was the best team in the world when I was a kid in the 90's and early 2000's. While Man United generally wouldn't stand a chance in with today's teams, at the time Man United were on an incredible run of good form and Man City were in a bit of a lull. That day, we woke up at 6 in the morning and took the train from London to Manchester, in a car full of drunk Mancunians from London, all wearing red and singing songs I'd learned to sing along with the crowds on TV when watching the matches from home. Arriving in Manchester was a surreal moment. Stepping onto the streets adorned with the iconic red hues, I felt an immediate connection with the city and its football heritage. The warm welcome from fellow fans and the distinctive Mancunian hospitality set the tone for an unforgettable adventure. The buzz around Old Trafford was palpable, and as we neared the Stretford End (where the diehard fans buy tickets), the stadium seemed to shake and the singing was almost deafening. The game itself was a rollercoaster of emotions. We left our individuality at the door and became the Stretford End. We yelled and cheered, sang and shouted all as one. Man U had the upper hand the whole first half but sadly Man City got the first goal. Saddened but not deflated we continued to shatter the air with our support until Man United scored an equalizer right in-front of our seats! Our stands errupted, hugged and kissed from all directious Old Trafford roared in support of our boys and began our songs again with unmatched ferver. Arm in arm the stadium swelled as United siezed the ball from City on the very next play, broke down the pitch toward us again and Marcus Rashford bagged the winning goal!!!! I felt as though I was walking in a dream. I'd could never have imagined I'd witness this victory nor just how much it would defy all my expectations. We floated back to the train station on a cloud to catch our train back to London. I sat down, and after all that emotion, I napped happily; dreaming my boyhood dream I'd just had come true.

Favorite Restaurant

Benny HaDayag - My grandmother used and I used to visit Benny HaDayag all the time in the years before she died. We would always get a table overlooking the harbor, order a couple of beers and a massive platter of mixed seafood to share, and sit for hours talking and eating together. More than most others I've met, my grandmother knew how to enjoy good food and good atmosphere; and Benny Hadayag gave me a place to truly enjoy that side of her.

Favorite Food

My favorite food in the world is Shawarma. The best Shawarma is always lamb served on laffa, with plenty of tahini, diced salad, french fries, and a good dollup of zhug (a hot type of sauce). Lamb shawarma on laffa with a generous drizzle of tahini, diced salad, french fries, and a kick from zhug – is a flavor explosion that makes the heart and taste buds sing in unison. Here's a take on my favorite shawarma:

Lamb Lovers' Paradise: Crafting the Ultimate Laffa Shawarma

In the realm of culinary ecstasy, nothing quite compares to the symphony of flavors found in my favorite dish – lamb shawarma served on warm, pillowy laffa. This gastronomic masterpiece is a celebration of the senses, each element carefully curated to create an unforgettable experience. For me, lamb is the undisputed star of the show. The tender, succulent slices of marinated lamb, slow-cooked to perfection, exude a richness that elevates the entire dish. The interplay of spices and the irresistible aroma as it grills are a prelude to the feast that awaits. The choice of laffa as the vessel is intentional. Its soft, slightly chewy texture serves as the perfect canvas to cradle the lamb and all its accompaniments. Warmed just right, it becomes the comforting embrace that wraps the entire creation. A shawarma without tahini is like a story without its climax. The velvety, nutty goodness of tahini adds a layer of creaminess that complements the robust lamb. It's not just a topping; it's the binding force that ties the entire ensemble together. The freshness of diced salad brings a burst of vibrancy to every bite. Crisp cucumbers, juicy tomatoes, and a medley of colorful vegetables provide a refreshing contrast, enhancing the overall experience and adding a delightful crunch. Ah, the unexpected hero – french fries nestled within the shawarma. Their golden crispiness introduces a playful element, a delightful surprise in every mouthful. It's a marriage of textures that takes the shawarma to new heights. No lamb shawarma is complete without zhug, the fiery hot sauce that ignites the taste buds. A dollop of zhug adds a bold, spicy kick, turning each bite into a flavorful adventure. It's the secret weapon that keeps me coming back for more. In every bite of this lamb shawarma on laffa, I savor not just a dish but a symphony of flavors – a culinary love affair that transcends the ordinary. It's not merely a meal; it's a journey, a celebration of the artistry that turns each ingredient into a masterpiece. This, my friend, is the epitome of shawarma perfection.
